How Optical Fibers Work

Optical fibers transmit data as pulses of light. The fiber core has a higher refractive index than its cladding, causing light to bounce along the core through total internal reflection.

This principle allows data to travel long distances with minimal loss, making fiber optics the ideal medium for high-speed, long-distance communication.
